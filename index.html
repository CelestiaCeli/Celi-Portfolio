AutoBiography

A game programmer and artist; Celeste Corbeil loves challenging tasks and being as dedicated as possible. A junior game developer with experience in C# - Unity, C++ - Unreal Engine, and GDScript - Godot Engine. Having done a few prototypes and collaborative projects.

Celeste’s Initial challenge was the education system and being socially introverted. She left the school system at a young age. Having self reflected during covid quarantine she managed to pull through adult education and applied to game programming college. Since then she has greatly worked on her skills such as socializing, programming, math, and art.

Her latest achievement was placing 2nd in the St Lawrence College 2025 Fall Game Jam having won a trophy. She has looked into Khan Academy and College for math and rediscovered love for it. She also loves spending her past time drawing and grows her skills every week on it.

Programming wise she has worked on a GitHub repository called “KitchenSinkGames” involving tools and software such as Hangman, Memory Matching, learning Vulkan, and more. She interprets several solutions and goes for the best way possible before heading into a problem and thus has a variety of prototypes and ideas. Her communication skills have been rapidly improved; focusing on being more direct and expressive.

Celeste’s enjoyment in gaming comes from a mix of passion between her love of technology and her early childhood dependency on social gaming due to be a social outcast at a young age. She appreciates how video games allows the opportunity for people struggling socialization to connect. Her turning point games were LittleBigPlanet 2 for it’s emphasis on sharing and expression in addition to Portal 2 showcasing how games are so much more than a small little toy but a world to immersive yourself similar to an interactive book.

Overall, Celeste’s goal is to make interestingly designed mechanics through modular coding and not give up across a cooperative team which contributes to an active yet engaging community. She desires to make expressive worlds and games that encourage word of mouth and solve intriguing logical puzzles with C#, and C++.
